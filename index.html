Working Paper

Academic Stress and Suicide Risk Among South Korean Adolescents (ongoing).

Supervisor: Professor David Sungho Park

2026
This study examines the impact of the start of the academic year, March 2nd on suicide deaths in Korean adolescents, aged 11 to 19. I exploit difference-in-difference method using data from Korea Microdata Integrated Service (MDIS) and Korean Statistical Information System (KOSIS). The treatment group consists of the individuals aged 11 to 19 "students", and the control group includes all other individuals in the dataset. I find a significant increase in suicide deaths among Korean students, aged 11 to 19 following March 2nd and the increase is not significant in population other than students. The result suggests that the start of the academic year in Korea on March 2nd every year triggers the increase in suicide rates among students.

Exploring Usability and User Value of the Electric Powered Wheelchair for the Elderly and Physically Disabled

with Professor Jaehyun Park

Proceedings of the 22nd Congress of the International Ergonomics Association, Volume 4

2025
Individuals experiencing various disabilities and aging who rely on Electric Powered Wheelchairs (EPW) for mobility assistance highlight the necessity of User-Centered Design in developing EPWs due to their diverse characteristics. This study evaluated the usability of a developed EPW, considering factors such as aging, prior EPW experience, and usage duration. Usability evaluations were conducted, including the System Usability Scale (SUS) and semi-structured interviews. Results indicate that elderly users rated the EPW more favorably than disabled users, with prior experience and usage duration influencing evaluations, suggesting a critical evaluation based on familiarity and robustness. Disabled users emphasized operational issues, especially regarding footrest height adjustment for long-term severe disability users, while elderly users appreciated its ease of operation. Longer EPW usage enhances user understanding, with previous device experience potentially influencing evaluations. These findings underscore the significance of considering user experience factors in EPW design for diverse user groups.

A Comprehensive Review Article on Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome and Role of Antioxidants in its Prevention and Treatment

Khalij-Libya Journal of Dental and Medical Research

2023
The emergence of novel coronaviruses, including the severe acute respiratory syndrome and the more recent COVID-19 pandemic caused by SARS-CoV-2, has captured global attention due to their dangerous nature and potential for international harm. This comprehensive review delves into the historical evolution, virology, and clinical intricacies of these viruses. Pivotal role of antioxidants, including vitamins A, C, D, and pivotal minerals such as zinc, copper, selenium, and magnesium, is meticulously examined in relation to their potential to bolster immune responses and counteract oxidative stress.
